About Madison

Madison Renee Hammel, Soprano

Madison Renee Hammel, Soprano, is a 2024 graduate of Oberlin College and Conservatory, earning her Bachelor of Music (Vocal Performance, studying under Professor Kendra Colton) and Bachelor of Arts in Psychology.

She performed with the Oberlin Chamber Singers and most recently sang the roles of Eurydice & La Musica in Monteverdi’s *L’Orfeo* and Ms. Wordsworth in Britten’s *Albert Herring* with the Oberlin Opera Theater.

She currently serves with the ArtistYear program as an AmeriCorps Resident Teaching Fellow at C.C.A. Baldi Middle School, where she supports Theater and Music classes and recently helped mount the school’s first musical production, *Moana Jr.*

Madison has performed in international choral festivals, including Alta Pusteria, Idaho, and Montana International Choral Festivals. She has also sung at Carnegie Hall, Thomaskirche in Leipzig, Tanglewood, the Kimmel Center, and the Academy of Music with the Pennsylvania Ballet.

Originally from Haddonfield, NJ, she graduated with honors from Haddonfield Memorial High School. She studied under Ms. Kristine Biller, sang with ChildrenSong of NJ under Ms. Polly Murray, and studied at Manhattan School of Music Pre-College with Ms. Jeanai La Vita.

Madison serves as a Cantor for parishes in the greater Philadelphia area, including Christ the King (Haddonfield, NJ), Saint James the Lesser (Jamesburg, NJ), and Queenship of Mary (Plainsboro, NJ). She is also an avid tennis player and dedicated home baker.
