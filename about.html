About Madison
Madison Renee Hammel, Soprano, is a 2024 graduate of Oberlin College and Conservatory, earning her Bachelor of Music (Vocal Performance, studying under Professor Kendra Colton) and Bachelor of Arts in Psychology. She performed with the Oberlin Chamber Singers and most recently sang the roles of Eurydice & La Musica in Monteverdi’s L’Orfeo and Ms. Wordsworth in Britten’s Albert Herring with the Oberlin Opera Theater.

Madison currently serves with the ArtistYear program as an Americorp Resident Teaching Fellow at C.C.A. Baldi Middle School, where she supports the teaching of their Theater and Music classes and recently assisted in putting on the school’s first Musical Theater production, Moana Jr.

Madison has performed in several international choral festivals,including Alta Pusteria International Choral Festival, Idaho International Choral Festival, and the Montana International Choral Festival. She has additionally performed at Carnegie Hall, Thomaskirche in Leipzig, Germany, Tanglewood as part of the Tanglewood Institute’s Young Artist Vocal Program, the Kimmel Center as part of the Harmony for Peace Foundation’s International Peace Day concerts, and the Academy of Music as part of the Pennsylvania Ballet’s performance of Carmina Burana.

Madison grew up in Haddonfield, New Jersey where she graduated with honors from Haddonfield Memorial High School. She studied voice and piano under Ms. Kristine Biller, and was a long-standing member of Childrensong of New Jersey under the artistic direction of founder Ms. Polly Murray. Madison additionally studied under Ms. Jeanai La Vita while attending Manhattan School of Music’s pre-college program.

Madison serves as a Cantor for several parishes in the greater Philadelphia area, including Christ the King Catholic Church in Haddonfield, NJ,James the Lesser Church in Jamesburg, NJ, and Queenship of Mary in Plainsboro, NJ. She continues to be an avid tennis player, as well as a dedicated home baker.
